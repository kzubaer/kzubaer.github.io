4. Isodesmic hydrogen-bonded supramolecular assembly of a phenylethynylene bis-urea macrocycle.

Summary: Herein, we investigate the hydrogen-bonded supramolecular polymerization of a phenylethynylene bis-urea macrocycle bearing a small linear solubilizing group. The macrocycle assembles in TCE and H2O/THF mixtures, with temperature-dependent UV-Vis and fluorescence spectroscopy revealing an isodesmic assembly mechanism. Thermodynamic analysis indicates an enthalpically-driven assembly with a moderate association constant in the 106 M-¹ range. Morphological studies, using dynamic light scattering and optical microscopy, revealed the formation of nanoscale aggregates and short fibrillar structures. We also probed the effect of end-capping additives. Minimal effects were observed at lower additive ratios, whereas 50% additives markedly reduced enthalpy and decreased the degree of polymerization, consistent with the chain-stopping effect. These results provide an understanding of the isodesmic assembly of phenylethynylene bis-urea macrocycle and show that competitive end-capping interactions can modulate the aggregation properties. (Preparing manuscript)
